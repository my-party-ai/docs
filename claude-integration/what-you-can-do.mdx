Once FastParty is connected to Claude, you can manage your entire event planning workflow through conversation. Here's everything Claude can do with FastParty.

## Create events

Tell Claude the basics and it creates the event in your FastParty account.

> "Create a birthday dinner for my daughter on July 5th at 7 PM. We're expecting 20 guests at our house."

## Update event details

Change any event field — name, date, time, location, description — just by asking.

> "Update my July 5th party — change the start time to 7:30 PM and add 'Black tie optional' to the description."

## Add guests and send invitations

Add one guest or many at once. Invitations go out automatically.

> "Add Sarah Johnson (sarah@email.com) and Mark Lee (mark@email.com) to my July 5th party and send them invitations."

## Check RSVP status

Get a live count or ask about specific guests.

> "How many people have RSVP'd to my July 5th party?"
> "Who hasn't responded yet?"

Claude can also send reminders to guests who haven't replied.

## Build and publish agendas

Create a full event timeline and publish it so guests can see it.

> "Set up an agenda for my party: welcome drinks at 7 PM for 30 minutes, dinner at 7:30 PM for 90 minutes, cake at 9 PM. Then publish it."

## Configure food and dietary preferences

Set up dietary options and enable guests to select them from their invitation.

> "Set up vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and nut-allergy options for my event and enable guests to select them."

## Create potluck sign-ups

Build a potluck sheet with categories and publish it for guests.

> "Create a potluck for my July 5th event with: Main Dishes (3 needed), Salads (2 needed), Desserts (3 needed), and Drinks (2 needed). Then publish it."

## Create and assign tasks

Add tasks to your pre-event checklist, with due dates and assignees.

> "Create these tasks: confirm headcount by June 28, order cake by July 1, buy decorations by July 3."

You can also update task status: "Mark the 'order cake' task as complete."

## Create guest circles

Save a group of people to reuse across future events.

> "Create a circle called 'Family' and add these people: Jane Smith (jane@email.com), Bob Smith (bob@email.com)."

---

<Tip>
  You can chain actions in a single message — "Create a birthday party for July 5th, add these three guests, and set up a vegetarian and vegan preference option" — and Claude will handle all of it in sequence.
</Tip>
